myDynasty

Privacy Policy

Effective May 19, 2026 · Last updated May 19, 2026 · App version 1.0 · Package com.mydynasty.app

Short version. myDynasty is a local-first family-tree app. All your family data — names, dates, photos, places — stays on your device in a private SQLite database.

We don't run an account system. We don't operate servers that receive your family data. We don't sell your data, use it for advertising, or share it with third parties for marketing. We don't embed third-party analytics, crash reporters, or trackers inside the app.

The only third-party SDKs that ever see anything are Google Maps SDK (when you open the map) and Google Play Billing (only if you subscribe to Premium). Both are operated by Google under Google's privacy policy.

If you uninstall the app, your data is removed from the device with it. We have no copy.

Scope of our controller role

We act as a data controller under GDPR Art 4(7) (and "оператор персональных данных" under Russian 152-ФЗ Art 3 п.2) in three narrow situations:

  1. Marketing-website visitor analytics on https://mydynasty.app, which currently uses Google Analytics 4 for visit counting (see §5).
  2. In-app integration of Google Maps SDK — we choose to use Maps; the user triggers it. Google itself is the controller of the underlying mapping service (joint-controller analysis under CJEU C-210/16 Fashion ID).
  3. In-app integration of Google Play Billing for the Premium subscription.

For the family data you enter into the app — names, dates, photos, places, biographical notes — we are not a controller and we are not a processor. That data is processed by you on your own device for personal and family purposes; it never reaches us. Under GDPR Art 2(2)(c) (household exemption) and Russian 152-ФЗ Art 1 ч.2 п.1, this means the data-protection regime treats you as the operator of that data, not us.

EU Representative (GDPR Art 27)

Because we are based outside the EU/EEA, GDPR Art 27 in principle requires us to appoint an EU representative. We currently rely on the exemption in Art 27(2)(a): the processing we actually carry out as a controller is occasional, does not include special-category data on our side (Art 9), and is low risk to data subjects. If this analysis no longer holds for you, please contact us at the email above.

2. What stays on your device only

The following information lives only in the app's local SQLite database in your private Android app sandbox. It is never sent to us:

  • Names, dates of birth and death, sex, biographical notes you enter for your relatives.
  • Family relationships you create (parent/child, spouse, sibling).
  • Photos and documents you import into the app archive (PNG, JPG, SVG, PDF, video, etc.).
  • GPS coordinates you attach to a person's birth, place of living, or burial.
  • Family-tree files you import (GEDCOM 7.0, LRV) or export (GEDCOM, LRV, SVG, PNG).
  • App preferences (theme, layout settings, locale).

Android isolates this database under your user profile. Other apps cannot read it without explicit permission. If you uninstall myDynasty, this data is deleted.

Special category data (GDPR Art 9)

Some of the data you may enter into the app — for example ethnicity, religion, health notes about ancestors — could qualify as "special category" personal data under GDPR Art 9. Because this data never leaves your device and we never receive or access it, we do not process such data within the meaning of GDPR or 152-ФЗ. The user themselves processes it under the household exemption.

myDynasty is not directed at children. The content, complexity, and subject matter are oriented toward adult users building genealogical records.

We do not knowingly process personal data of children below the age of digital consent in their jurisdiction:

Users who upload photos or data about identifiable third parties (including minors) are responsible for any consent required under their local law. The household / personal-use exemption (GDPR Art 2(2)(c)) generally applies while the data is kept for personal/family purposes; it ceases the moment the user shares the data publicly (e.g. by exporting GEDCOM and publishing it).

If you are a parent or guardian and want such content removed, simply delete the records inside the app (everything is local) or uninstall the app.

8. Data security

  • All app data is stored in your device's private application sandbox, isolated by the Android operating system.
  • Stored at rest in the OS-encrypted application sandbox — Android full-disk / file-based encryption applies on devices with a screen lock enabled (default for modern devices). We rely on this OS-level protection.
  • No additional app-layer encryption on top of OS encryption in v1.0. We say this honestly. We plan to evaluate SQLCipher / Android-keystore-protected database encryption in a future release.
  • We recommend that you protect your device itself with a PIN, password, or biometric lock — this is the strongest practical protection for a local-first app.
  • Network traffic to Google services is performed over HTTPS by the respective Google SDKs.
